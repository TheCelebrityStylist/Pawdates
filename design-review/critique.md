# Tailtend design review

## Round 1 — desktop landing

- Ownability: the treatment-record concept, collar tag, mono facts and paper texture no longer read as a generic dark SaaS template.
- Signature hierarchy: the red stamp is the only expressive graphic; green remains functional.
- Type/materials: display, UI and record data have distinct roles; ledger rules and warm shadows are consistent.
- Rhythm: hero, proof line, full record sheet, loop, email and ledger list alternate density rather than repeating one card grid.
- Issue found: the hero headline exceeded its desktop column measure. Fixed by narrowing it to 10 characters and reducing the responsive maximum.
- Chanel pass: removed the forced headline line break; the text now wraps naturally within a deliberate measure.

## Round 2 — 390px landing and login

- Touch targets remain at least 44px and CTAs wrap without horizontal overflow.
- The live record remains legible and visually distinct below the hero.
- Login now has a meaningful brand surface instead of a floating card in a void.
- Issue found: the letter inside the dark-panel collar tag inherited white and disappeared. Fixed with an explicit ink color.
- Stamp interaction tested: one unique action changes the treatment row to `DONE · 12 JUL 2026` and advances the button copy.
- Chanel pass: removed the extra hero accent treatment; the headline stays ink-only so the stamp remains the boldest moment.

## Round 3 — desktop login and final system pass

- The dark login panel is the only large dark surface and carries a clear night-before-the-vet-record meaning.
- Static and interactive stamps share one construction and never compete with unrelated decoration.
- Dates, record numbers, countdowns, trust facts and metadata use IBM Plex Mono; prose remains Instrument Sans.
- Cards exist only where they represent a physical artifact: treatment record, email or price sheet.
- Mobile and desktop builds pass without overflow or type errors.
- Chanel pass: retained only one miniature record on login; no additional illustration or decorative pet imagery was added.

Final checklist: full pass. No further accessory improves the concept.
